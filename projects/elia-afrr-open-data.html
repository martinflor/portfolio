Context

This post is a first pass at exploring automatic Frequency Restoration Reserve (aFRR) using ELIA Open Data. The goal is to extract bidding-relevant signals from historical behavior: volume-weighted prices, acceptance probabilities, and a simple historical bid recommendation approach.

Data sources

aFRR energy prices

Energy activation prices come from two datasets (dataset switch in May 2024):

  • ods064 — until 2024-05-21
  • ods166 — from 2024-05-22 onward

aFRR capacity prices & awarded volumes

Capacity market results come from:

  • ods125 — awarded capacity prices & awarded volumes
Market rule that matters

aFRR capacity prices are pay-as-bid (not pay-as-cleared). Each accepted bid is paid at its own bid price — which makes acceptance probability and bid selection central to strategy design.

Volume-weighted capacity prices

Capacity prices can be noisy if awarded volumes vary. A robust way to summarize “what the system actually paid” is the volume-weighted capacity price:

\[ P_{\text{vw}} = \frac{\sum_i P_i \cdot V_i}{\sum_i V_i} \]

where \(P_i\) is the awarded capacity price and \(V_i\) the awarded volume (within the same time bucket and direction). In this figure, UP and DOWN are shown together (so no toggle is needed).

Probability of being accepted vs bidding price

With pay-as-bid, the key question becomes: “If I bid at price \(p\), what is my probability of acceptance?”

We estimate: \[ \mathbb{P}(\text{accept} \mid p) \] and segment by time blocks (e.g. 00–04, 04–08, …). Toggle below to compare UP vs DOWN acceptance behavior.

Recommended bid using historical expected value

Acceptance probability alone isn’t enough: a very low bid might be accepted almost always, but yields little value. A simple historical decision rule is to maximize a proxy for expected value:

\[ \text{EV}(p) = p \cdot \mathbb{P}(\text{accept} \mid p) \]

The baseline “recommended bid” per time block (and per direction) is the \(p\) that maximizes \(\text{EV}(p)\), then adjusted based on risk appetite (prefer acceptance vs prefer price).

Practical workflow (baseline)

1) Estimate acceptance curves per time block

Compute acceptance frequencies over bid-price bins (or fit a smooth curve).

2) Compute \(\text{EV}(p)\) and identify \(p^\*\)

Pick the price that maximizes \(\text{EV}(p)=p\cdot\mathbb{P}(\text{accept}\mid p)\) per block/direction.

3) Apply a risk adjustment

Bid slightly below \(p^\*\) for higher acceptance, or above \(p^\*\) if you accept more misses for higher unit price.

Important note

This is not a forecast. It’s a historical decision-support baseline. Market conditions change, so curves should be refreshed regularly and ideally conditioned on system/tightness indicators.

Limitations & next steps

Limitations

  • Assumes historical stationarity.
  • Doesn’t model structural changes explicitly.
  • No participant-level strategy decomposition.

Next steps

  • Add DOWN-side commentary symmetry/asymmetry.
